## How the Algorithm Works (2026)

- First **1.5–3 seconds** decides if the Reel gets shown to non-followers
- Instagram ranks on: **watch time % > saves > shares > comments > likes**
- Short Reels (7–30s) get highest completion rate; longer Reels (30–60s) need to earn every second
- Trending audio gives ~1.5x algorithmic boost — check trending tab before filming each week
- Instagram actively **suppresses** generic AI-generated or templated content — show your face, your screen, real results
- Best posting window: **8–10am** or **8–11pm** local time
- CTA placement: put it in the **middle** of the Reel, not just at the end

## The 5 Patterns Behind Every Viral Reel

*(from reverse-engineering thousands of high-performing Reels)*

### 1. POV Format
"POV: You finally understand [concept]."
- Immediately relatable, high share rate
- Works best for moments of realisation
- **AI niche version:** "POV: You're a complete beginner who just trained their first ML model."

### 2. Authority Hook
Opens with a bold claim that makes the viewer self-select.
- "Most people learning AI are wasting their time on the wrong things."
- Filters audience — people who identify stay and watch
- Best for educational content with a counterintuitive angle

### 3. Micro-Story Loop
Short story with unresolved tension at the start → resolution at the end.
- "I spent 3 hours debugging. Turned out to be one missing comma." → resolution
- Drives completion rate and replays
- The viewer needs to see how it ends

### 4. Transformation Reveal
Before → after with a clear, measurable gap.
- "Week 1 vs. Week 8 of learning Python" (side-by-side code screenshots)
- "My dashboard on Day 1 vs. Day 30"
- Naturally drives completion — people watch to see the after

### 5. Open Loop Hook
Creates a question in the viewer's mind they can only answer by watching.
- "I wish someone told me this before I started learning AI."
- "The one mistake that slowed my AI learning by 2 months."
- Never answer the hook in the first 3 seconds

## Video Format Templates

### Format 1: The Day-N Update (your primary format, Months 1–2)
```
[0:00–0:02] Text on screen: "Day [N] of learning AI from zero."
[0:02–0:05] Talking head: "Here's what happened this week."
[0:05–0:30] Screen recording: show what you built/learned
[0:30–0:45] Talking head: "This is what surprised me / what I got wrong."
[0:45–0:55] Text card: "Week [N+1] I'm going to [specific goal]."
[0:55–1:00] "Follow to see if I actually do it."
```
**Why it works:** Creates a series people feel invested in. They want to see the outcome.

### Format 2: The Revelation (Month 3+)
```
[0:00–0:03] Bold claim on screen + voice: "You don't need [X] to get an AI job."
[0:03–0:08] "Here's what you actually need instead."
[0:08–0:40] 3 points, each on screen as text + spoken
[0:40–0:50] "I'm proving this on my own account — follow to see."
[0:50–1:00] "Save this if you're learning AI too."
```
**Why it works:** Authority hook + actionable content + direct tie to your own journey.

### Format 3: The Screen Recording Tutorial
```
[0:00–0:03] "Watch me [build/deploy/fix] this in 60 seconds."
[0:03–0:55] Screen recording with voiceover narration + text callouts
[0:55–1:00] "Link in bio to see the full thing live."
```
**Why it works:** Visual proof of competence. "Show don't tell."

### Format 4: The Before/After
```
[0:00–0:03] "Week 1 vs. Week [N]."
[0:03–0:20] Show Week 1 (blank/beginner state)
[0:20–0:50] Show Week N (current progress)
[0:50–1:00] "If I can do this starting from zero, you can too. Follow."
```
**Why it works:** Transformation stories drive saves and shares.

### Format 5: The Failure Reel (counter-intuitive — high engagement)
```
[0:00–0:03] "I tried to [thing]. It broke. Here's what happened."
[0:03–0:45] Honest walkthrough of what went wrong
[0:45–0:55] "Here's what I actually learned from it."
[0:55–1:00] "Week [N+1] I'm going to fix this. Follow to see."
```
**Why it works:** Vulnerability drives comments. People rally around authentic failure.

## Text/Caption Formats That Drive Saves

### The Save-Bait Ending (add to every Reel)
Last text card, white on dark background, large font:
> **"Save this so you don't forget it."**

Saves are the highest-value engagement signal in 2026. Every Reel should have this card.

### Caption Formula
```
Line 1: Restate the hook as a statement (not a question)
Line 2-4: 2-3 key points from the video as bullet points
Line 5: CTA — "Follow for week [N+1]" or "Comment [word] and I'll send you [thing]"
.
.
.
[hashtags on separate lines or at very end]
```

### Comment CTA That Works
"Comment **AI** if you're also learning from scratch → I'll DM you my full 6-month plan"
This drives DMs which are the highest engagement signal Instagram tracks.

## Weekly Research System (20 min every Sunday)

### Step 1 — Competitor harvest
5 accounts to study (2K–50K followers, AI/tech learning niche):
- Search: `#ailearning`, `#codingjourney`, `#learningpython`, `#aifromzero`
- For each account: sort Reels by views. Top 3 Reels = your research material
- For each viral Reel, log in `research-log.md`:
  - First 1.5 seconds verbatim (the hook)
  - Format (talking head / screen recording / text only / voiceover)
  - Topic category (personal update / tutorial / revelation / failure / before-after)
  - Hook type (number / contradiction / question / POV / story)

### Step 2 — Trending audio
- Open Instagram → Reels tab → tap audio on any Reel → save 3 with trending arrow
- Check once per week, not daily

### Step 3 — Your own analytics (Meta Business Suite)
After every post, check:
- Reach from non-followers (did it get distributed?)
- Watch-through rate (did people watch to the end?)
- Saves (did people find it worth keeping?)

### Step 4 — Save your own scroll stops
When you stop scrolling on a Reel this week, save it. Sunday: review all saved Reels. Ask: what made me stop? Log the hook.

## Automation — What's Possible vs. What's Not

### Safe to automate (won't get your account banned)
| Task | Tool | How |
|------|------|-----|
| Schedule posts in advance | **Later.com** (free tier) | Upload Reel + caption + hashtags, set time |
| Pull your own follower count into dashboard | **Instagram Graph API** | Python script, requires business account |
| Get your own post analytics | **Instagram Graph API** | Python script, reads insights |
| Generate weekly digest from your week-N.md | **Anthropic API** | Already in Month 4 plan |
| Batch-write captions for next week | **Claude API** | Feed your hook + bullet points, get caption |

### Not safe (violates ToS, risks ban)
| Task | Why avoid |
|------|-----------|
| Auto-follow/unfollow | Detected by Instagram, account flagged |
| Auto-like/comment at scale | Same — instant red flag |
| Scraping competitor accounts programmatically | Violates ToS |
| Using third-party growth bots (UpGrow, etc.) | Violation risk, fake engagement |

### Instagram Graph API setup (for your own account)
Requirements:
1. Convert your Instagram account to **Creator** or **Business** (free, Settings → Account type)
2. Create a Facebook page (required, takes 5 min)
3. Connect Instagram to the Facebook page
4. Create a free Meta Developer app at developers.facebook.com
5. Get your **access token** and **Instagram Business Account ID**

What you can then do with a Python script:
```python
import requests

# Pull your follower count
url = f"https://graph.instagram.com/{IG_ACCOUNT_ID}?fields=followers_count&access_token={TOKEN}"
data = requests.get(url).json()
followers = data['followers_count']

# Write to progress.json so dashboard updates automatically
import json
with open('progress.json', 'r+') as f:
    progress = json.load(f)
    progress['instagram']['followers'] = followers
    f.seek(0)
    json.dump(progress, f)
```

This script + a cron job = your dashboard automatically shows your real follower count. We build this in Month 2 when you learn Python file I/O.
